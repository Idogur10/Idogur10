Who I Am

Research-Driven
Engineer

I'm a deep learning engineer completing my M.Sc. in Biomedical Engineering at Ben Gurion University, where I work in the Biomedical Robotics Lab under Professors Ilana Nisky and Nir Shlezinger.

My research applies deep neural networks to teleoperated robotic surgery — using 3D kinematic data to predict and optimize surgeon hand movements in real time. The resulting model outperforms standard baselines and contributes to safer, more precise surgical robotics.

Beyond my thesis, I have led multidisciplinary projects in brain-computer interfaces, haptic feedback systems, and EEG signal processing, combining rigorous engineering with real-world clinical impact.
